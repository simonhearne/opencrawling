# Trademark Policy

This document outlines the policy of the **OpenCrawling Organization** regarding the use of our trademarks, service marks, logos, and brand identifiers (collectively, the **"Marks"**).

The primary Mark covered by this policy is:
*   **OpenCrawling®**

While the source code for our projects (including the OpenCrawling framework and the Open Ingestion Standard) is licensed under the open-source **Apache License 2.0**, that license does **not** grant any rights to use our Marks. This Trademark Policy governs the use of our Marks to protect the reputation, security, and integrity of the OpenCrawling project.

---

## 1. Allowed Uses (No Permission Required)

You may use the **OpenCrawling** name under the following "nominative fair use" conditions without seeking prior written consent, provided that such use is truthful, non-misleading, and does not imply sponsorship or endorsement:

*   **Accurate References:** You may use the name to refer to the official OpenCrawling project, website, or software (e.g., *"We use OpenCrawling for our enterprise data ingestion pipeline"*).
*   **Compatibility Statements:** You may state that your own software or service is compatible with OpenCrawling, provided you use a descriptive phrase such as *"compatible with OpenCrawling®"* or *"works with OpenCrawling®"*. Do not use phrases like *"OpenCrawling Core"* or *"OpenCrawling Edition"*.
*   **Forks and Modifications:** If you fork or modify the software under the Apache License 2.0, you may state that your work is based on OpenCrawling (e.g., *"This project is a fork of OpenCrawling®"*), subject to the rebranding requirements in Section 3.

---

## 2. Prohibited Uses (Permission Required)

You may not use our Marks in the following ways without explicit, prior written permission from the OpenCrawling Organization:

*   **Official Endorsement or Affiliation:** You must not use the Marks in a way that implies the OpenCrawling Organization sponsors, endorses, or is affiliated with your product, service, company, or organization.
*   **Product and Company Names:** You must not incorporate the Marks into your company name, product name, service name, or domain names (e.g., `opencrawling-support.com` or `enterpriseopencrawling.org` are prohibited).
*   **Logo Alteration:** You must not modify, distort, or change the color or structure of the official OpenCrawling logo.

---

## 3. Requirements for Forks and Custom Distributions

If you choose to distribute a modified version of the OpenCrawling codebase (e.g., a fork):

1.  **Rebrand the Product:** You must choose a new, distinct name for your project, product, and executable files. You may not use "OpenCrawling" in the name of your distribution.
2.  **Remove Branding Assets:** You must remove the official OpenCrawling logos and trademarks from your user interface, documentation, and promotional materials.
3.  **Acknowledge the Source:** You may include a truthful statement indicating that your project is a fork or derivative of OpenCrawling (e.g., *"This product is based on OpenCrawling®"*).

---

## 4. How to Ask for Permission

If you wish to use the OpenCrawling Marks in a manner not explicitly permitted by this policy, please contact us by opening an issue on our [Community repository](https://github.com/OpenCrawling/opencrawling/issues) or reaching out directly to the project maintainers. We review requests on a case-by-case basis.

---

Whenever you use our Marks, please include the following attribution statement where appropriate (e.g., in the footer of your website, in legal notices, or in your project README):
